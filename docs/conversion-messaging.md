# Conversion Messaging System

> Messaging and narrative governance for LIFETIME product pages and buy modules.  
> This document exclusively controls **copy and messaging** – no UI, no layout, no code.

---

## Language Rule

All system rules are written in English.

All user-facing copy MUST be:
- German (Du-form)
- aligned with the defined tone guidelines

English may be used only for internal logic or when explicitly required.

---

## 1. Authority & Scope

### What this document controls

- Messaging logic within existing buy modules  
- Decision guidance through copy  
- Positioning of subscription vs. one-time purchase  
- Value communication and claim hierarchy  
- Tone in purchase-relevant contexts  

### What this document does NOT control

- Layout, spacing, typography, colors → `/docs/design-governance.md`  
- Shopify structure and functionality → unchanged  
- Component logic, CSS, backend → outside of scope  
- Medical claims or product truths → compliance first  

### Hierarchy

1. Product truth and compliance override any messaging preference  
2. `/docs/design-governance.md` controls all visual decisions  
3. This document only controls **what is said** – not how it looks  
4. Shopify architecture remains untouched  

---

## 2. Core Conversion Narrative

### Guiding principle

Longevity is created through consistency – not through isolated actions.

### Translation for the user

| Principle | What the user understands |
|---|---|
| Consistency beats intensity | “A small step every day does more than occasional effort." |
| Routine is key | “If it becomes part of my daily life, it works." |
| Continuous support | “My body needs regular support, not occasional." |

### Boundaries

- No medical cure claims  
- No exaggeration of effects or timelines  
- No comparisons with medication or therapies  

---

## 3. Decision Framing

### Subscription

**Role:** Designed for long-term routine and continuity.

| Dimension | Messaging focus |
|---|---|
| Routine | “Becomes part of your daily routine automatically." |
| Continuity | “Seamless supply without thinking." |
| Benefit | “Save [X] % compared to one-time purchase." |
| Control | “Adjust, pause, or cancel anytime." |

**Primary message (one only):** Effortless continuity.

### One-time purchase

**Role:** The flexible entry without commitment.

| Dimension | Messaging focus |
|---|---|
| Flexibility | “Order once, try without commitment." |
| Entry | “Ideal to get to know the product." |
| Freedom | “No subscription, no follow-up deliveries." |

**Primary message (one only):** Flexible entry.

### Rules

- Each option has **one** clearly defined role  
- The same message must not appear in both options  
- Avoid internal marketing language (no “our bestseller”, no “customer favorite”)  
- The choice must feel like a real decision, not an upsell trick  

---

## 4. Claim Hierarchy

### Principle

Each option, badge, or label communicates **exactly one statement**. No stacking.

### Forbidden: Claim stacking

```
❌ “Recommended" + “Most chosen" + “Most popular" + “Bestseller"
```

### Allowed: One badge, one purpose

| Badge | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Recommendation | Provide orientation | “Recommended" |
| Savings | Quantify price advantage | “Save 15 %" |
| Popularity | Social proof | “Most chosen" |

**Rule:** Maximum one badge per option.

---

## 5. Allowed Messaging Types

### Allowed

- decision framing  
- value explanation  
- savings  
- reassurance  
- control/flexibility  
- routine/continuity  

### Forbidden

- vague hype  
- duplicate claims  
- empty superlatives  
- redundant labels  
- unclear recommendations  

---

## 6. Buy Module – Messaging Logic

### Step 1: Pack selection

- Differentiate via quantity, duration, or value  
- Each option should imply a usage horizon  

### Step 2: Subscription vs. one-time purchase

- Clearly separate continuity vs. flexibility  
- State savings clearly  

### Step 3: CTA support text

CTA support text should:
- reinforce the decision  
- reduce last-minute friction  
- avoid introducing new complexity  

Prefer:
- reassurance (e.g. delivery, cancellation)  
- clarity (what happens next)  

Avoid:
- new arguments  
- new product information  

### Step 4: Reassurance elements

- Maximum 3–4 elements  
- Each solves one concern  

---

## 7. Tone Balance: Science × Everyday Life

### Target perception

“This is credible – and I understand it immediately.”

### Rules

Avoid:
- technical jargon  
- study-heavy language  

Prefer:
- simple cause-effect  
- concrete benefits  

Tone:
- intelligent but simple  
- calm and trustworthy  
- modern but grounded
