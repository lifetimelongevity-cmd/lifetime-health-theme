# PDP Architecture System (LIFETIME)

> Defines the structure, psychology, and decision logic of all product pages.  
> This document controls **what is built and in which order** – not design, not code.

---

## 1. PDP Goal

The PDP must convert a **skeptical, semi-informed visitor** into a **confident buyer**.

Final user state:
- Understands what the product does
- Feels personally addressed
- Trusts the product quality and brand
- Perceives low risk
- Has a clear reason to buy now

---

## 2. Customer Context

Traffic is primarily:
- Cold to semi-warm (Meta, Google Ads)
- Limited prior knowledge about NMN / NAD⁺
- Skeptical towards supplements

Implications:
- Reduce complexity
- Avoid technical overload
- Build trust early
- Focus on clarity > completeness

---

## 3. Psychological Journey

The PDP must guide the user through these stages in order:

1. Attention → “This is relevant for me”
2. Understanding → “I get what this does”
3. Personal relevance → “This helps me specifically”
4. Trust → “This is credible and high quality”
5. Risk reduction → “Safe to try”
6. Action → “I should buy now”

Each section must serve ONE of these stages.

---

## 4. Section Architecture (NMN PDP)

### 1. Hero + Buybox
Goal: Immediate clarity + purchase option  
Status: Already built

---

### 2. Benefit Compression
Goal: Make value instantly understandable

Content:
- 3–4 core benefits
- Outcome-focused (not ingredients)
- максимально einfach formuliert

---

### 3. Trust + Mechanism Bridge
Goal: Justify WHY the product works

Content:
- Simple explanation (no deep science)
- NAD⁺ as central concept (simplified)
- First trust signals (quality, science, expertise)

---

### 4. Social Proof
Goal: Reduce uncertainty through others

Content:
- Reviews / testimonials
- Optional: quantified signals (ratings, volume)

---

### 5. Product Quality & Differentiation
Goal: Explain why THIS product

Content:
- Purity
- Testing / lab validation
- Bioavailability
- Clear differentiation vs. market

---

### 6. Usage & Routine
Goal: Make usage feel easy and realistic

Content:
- How to take
- When to take
- Integration into daily routine

---

### 7. FAQ (Conversion Block)
Goal: Remove final objections

Content:
- Safety
- Who is it for
- Timing of effects
- Combination with other products

---

### 8. Final CTA
Goal: Trigger decision

Content:
- Clear, low-friction call to action
- Reinforce trust + simplicity

---

## 5. Core Principles

- One section = one job
- Clarity beats completeness
- Avoid scientific overload
- Always translate to user benefit
- Build trust early and continuously
- Reduce friction before asking for action

---

## 6. Rules for Building Sections

When creating a new section:

- It MUST fit into the defined structure
- It MUST serve one psychological stage
- It MUST not duplicate previous sections
- It MUST be understandable within 3–5 seconds

---

## 7. What NOT to do

- No random sections
- No feature stacking without context
- No deep scientific explanations
- No repeating the same argument twice
- No design-first thinking

---

## 8. Execution Workflow

For each new section:

1. Identify current position in journey
2. Define section goal (1 sentence)
3. Create copy (German, Du-form)
4. Define structure (blocks, hierarchy)
5. Then implement in code
